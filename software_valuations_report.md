## Key Themes

1. **SaaS commanded the highest revenue multiple in every single year** — peaking at 30x in 2021, bottoming at 11x in 2022, recovering to ~14x by 2024.
2. **2022 was the great compression year** — all three segments halved or worse on EV/Revenue as the Fed hiked rates 425bp. Consumer was hit hardest in absolute terms.
3. **Enterprise was most defensible** — smallest peak-to-trough swing (17x → 9x vs. SaaS's 30x → 12x) due to more visible revenue and stronger cash generation.
4. **Consumer never recovered** — the 2024 median of 5.4x is the same as 2022 lows, while SaaS and Enterprise both recovered meaningfully.
5. **The AI premium is real but concentrated** — Palantir's 2024 EV/Revenue of 59.5x is a strong outlier; excluding it, the Enterprise median drops to 10.8x.
6. **EV/EBITDA medians are less reliable** for Consumer and early-stage SaaS due to high NM rates and near-zero EBITDA distortions. Enterprise is the most meaningful cohort for EBITDA-based valuation.
